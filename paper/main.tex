\begin{abstract}
Dimensional Aspect Sentiment Quad Prediction (DimASQP) requires extracting quadruplets of aspect terms, opinion expressions, aspect categories, and continuous Valence-Arousal (VA) scores from text. Its official metric, Continuous F1 (cF1), directly penalizes VA prediction errors through Euclidean distance weighting---making VA accuracy not an auxiliary concern but a core component of the task objective. Yet current token-pair matrix methods predict VA at the \emph{token level}, creating a fundamental granularity mismatch: evaluation is performed per \emph{quadruplet}, but prediction is anchored to individual tokens that may participate in multiple quads with different VA values. Even span-pair conditioning, which resolves this ambiguity, still treats aspect and opinion symmetrically and fails to exploit the strong sentiment prior carried by opinion expressions. We propose \textbf{Opinion-Guided VA prediction}, which decomposes VA estimation into an opinion-derived prior---capturing the base sentiment inherent in the opinion expression---and a gated context residual that adjusts for aspect-specific modulation. Controlled experiments on DimABSA 2026 English benchmarks show that this decomposition significantly reduces VA prediction error on both restaurant (Euclidean distance $-$9.6\%, $p<0.001$) and laptop ($-$11.8\%, $p<0.05$) domains while maintaining or improving extraction performance. Cross-domain analysis reveals that VA quality gains are consistent, though the interaction with end-to-end cF1 is domain-sensitive---providing a nuanced picture of when and why opinion-guided decomposition helps.

\keywords{Dimensional Sentiment Analysis \and Valence-Arousal \and Aspect Sentiment Quad Prediction \and Opinion-Guided Learning}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA) aims to identify fine-grained sentiment toward specific aspects in text~\cite{pontiki2014semeval,pontiki2016semeval,zhang2022survey}. Conventional ABSA represents sentiment as discrete polarities (positive, negative, neutral), but this categorical view cannot distinguish ``\emph{amazing}'' from ``\emph{acceptable}''---both positive, yet fundamentally different in emotional intensity. Dimensional Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (DimABSA)~\cite{dimabsa2026} addresses this by adopting the Valence-Arousal (VA) model from affective psychology~\cite{russell1980circumplex}, replacing polarity labels with two continuous scores: \emph{valence} ($V \in [1,9]$, pleasantness) and \emph{arousal} ($A \in [1,9]$, activation).

The most demanding formulation in this direction is \textbf{Dimensional Aspect Sentiment Quad Prediction (DimASQP)}, which requires extracting quadruplets $(a, c, o, \langle V, A \rangle)$---aspect term, aspect category, opinion expression, and continuous VA values. Crucially, the official evaluation metric Continuous F1 (cF1)~\cite{dimabsa2026} \emph{directly} penalizes VA prediction errors: each correctly extracted quadruplet receives a score discounted by the Euclidean distance between predicted and gold VA values (Eq.~\ref{eq:ctp}). A system that perfectly extracts all quads but predicts VA poorly will receive a severely degraded cF1. This makes VA prediction quality not an auxiliary concern, but a \emph{first-class component of the task objective}.

Despite this, existing token-pair matrix methods~\cite{gao2023oneasqp} treat VA prediction as a secondary output, predicting VA at the \emph{token level}---a design that creates a fundamental misalignment with the task:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Granularity mismatch.} cF1 evaluates VA per \emph{quadruplet}, but token-level prediction is anchored to individual positions. When a token participates in multiple quads with different VA values (e.g., ``\emph{food}'' in both a quality and a price quad), a single token-level value cannot correctly serve all of them. The prediction unit does not match the evaluation unit.
    \item \textbf{Missing sentiment grounding.} In most quadruplets, the opinion expression is the primary carrier of VA information---``\emph{terrible}'' implies low valence regardless of what aspect it modifies, while the aspect mainly modulates intensity. Yet token-level heads process positions independently, with no mechanism to ground prediction in the expression that most directly determines VA.
\end{enumerate}

A natural remedy is to condition VA on the specific aspect-opinion span pair, resolving the granularity mismatch. However, as we show empirically (Sec.~\ref{sec:ablation}), simply concatenating span representations yields only marginal improvement---the model still treats aspect and opinion symmetrically and must learn the opinion-VA relationship implicitly. The key missing piece is an explicit inductive bias that reflects a structural asymmetry in the task: \emph{opinion expressions are the primary VA anchors; aspects are modulators}.

We propose \textbf{Opinion-Guided VA prediction}, a mechanism that directly encodes this asymmetry. It decomposes VA estimation into: (1)~an \emph{opinion prior} derived solely from the opinion span, providing a base VA estimate grounded in the sentiment-carrying expression; and (2)~a \emph{context residual} from the aspect-opinion interaction, capturing how the specific aspect modulates the opinion's base sentiment. A learnable gate balances the two components, allowing the model to adaptively control how much of the final prediction comes from the opinion prior versus the contextual adjustment. This design is not an arbitrary architectural choice, but a direct response to the structure of the DimASQP task: it aligns the VA prediction unit with the quadruplet-level evaluation, and it grounds VA in the linguistically appropriate unit---the opinion expression.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item We identify a granularity misalignment between token-level VA prediction and quadruplet-level cF1 evaluation in DimASQP, and propose Opinion-Guided VA prediction---a gated prior-residual mechanism that grounds VA estimation in the opinion expression within a one-stage extraction framework.
    \item We conduct a three-stage ablation (token-level $\to$ span-pair $\to$ opinion-guided) demonstrating that resolving the granularity mismatch alone is insufficient; explicitly modeling the opinion prior is the key factor driving statistically significant VA quality improvements ($p<0.001$).
    \item We provide interpretable evidence for \emph{why} the method works: learned gate analysis reveals a stable 60:40 residual-to-prior ratio across seeds, and per-category breakdowns show that high-variability categories benefit most from the opinion prior.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Aspect Sentiment Quad Prediction}

Aspect Sentiment Quad Prediction (ASQP)~\cite{cai2021acos} requires extracting all $(a, c, o, s)$ quadruplets from text, where $s$ denotes sentiment polarity. Approaches fall into two paradigms: \emph{generative methods} that formulate ASQP as sequence-to-sequence generation~\cite{zhang2021paraphrase,mao2022seq2path,gou2023mvp}, and \emph{extractive methods} that use structured prediction over token pairs~\cite{gao2023oneasqp}. One-ASQP~\cite{gao2023oneasqp} introduced a token-pair matrix approach with EfficientGlobalPointer~\cite{su2022globalpointer}, decomposing quad extraction into parallel sub-tasks. Our work extends this extractive framework to the dimensional setting.

\subsection{Dimensional Sentiment Analysis}

The shift from categorical to dimensional sentiment representations~\cite{warriner2013norms,mohammad2018obtaining} has gained momentum with shared tasks such as SIGHAN 2024 dimABSA and SemEval-2026 DimABSA~\cite{dimabsa2026}. HITSZ-HLT~\cite{hitszSighan2024} achieved top performance at SIGHAN 2024 with a hybrid BERT-LLM approach. CL-Span~\cite{clspanSighan2024} enhanced span representations with contrastive learning for VA prediction. Recent SemEval-2026 systems predominantly adopt LLM-based approaches, such as SCSG~\cite{scsg2026} using structured generation with self-consistency. While LLM-based systems generally lead the shared task leaderboard, our work focuses on improving the \emph{VA prediction component} within encoder-based extractors, complementary to advances in extraction architecture.

\subsection{Opinion-Sentiment Relationship}

The intuition that opinion expressions carry strong sentiment signals is well-established in ABSA. Opinion term extraction has been studied as an auxiliary task that benefits aspect-level sentiment classification~\cite{li2019unified,fan2019target,chen2020synchronous}. However, the explicit use of opinion representations as \emph{sentiment priors} for continuous VA prediction has not been explored. Our opinion-guided mechanism provides a principled way to leverage this intuition in the dimensional setting.

\section{Problem Definition}
\label{sec:problem}

Given an input text $\mathbf{x} = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$, the DimASQP task requires extracting a set of quadruplets $\mathcal{Q} = \{(a_i, c_i, o_i, \langle V_i, A_i \rangle)\}_{i=1}^{|\mathcal{Q}|}$, where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $a_i$ is an aspect term (a text span from $\mathbf{x}$, or \texttt{NULL} if implicit),
    \item $c_i \in \mathcal{C}$ is an aspect category from a predefined taxonomy,
    \item $o_i$ is an opinion expression (a text span, or \texttt{NULL} if implicit),
    \item $V_i \in [1, 9]$ and $A_i \in [1, 9]$ are valence and arousal scores.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation.}\quad The official metric is Continuous F1 (cF1). Predicted and gold quadruplets are matched by exact string match on $(a, c, o)$. For each matched pair, a continuous true-positive score is computed as:
\begin{equation}
    \text{cTP} = \max\left(0,\; 1 - \frac{\sqrt{(v_p - v_g)^2 + (a_p - a_g)^2}}{\sqrt{128}}\right)
    \label{eq:ctp}
\end{equation}
where $\sqrt{128}$ is the maximum Euclidean distance in the VA space.
cF1 is the harmonic mean of
$\text{cPrec} = \frac{\sum \text{cTP}}{|\text{TP}| + |\text{FP}|}$
and
$\text{cRec} = \frac{\sum \text{cTP}}{|\text{TP}| + |\text{FN}|}$.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Overview}

Our framework builds upon the One-ASQP~\cite{gao2023oneasqp} architecture, extending it with a dedicated VA prediction head for dimensional sentiment estimation. As illustrated in Fig.~\ref{fig:architecture}, the system consists of three components: (1)~a pre-trained encoder that produces contextualized token representations; (2)~a token-pair matrix head that jointly extracts aspect terms, opinion expressions, and aspect categories; and (3)~an Opinion-Guided VA head that predicts continuous VA values conditioned on extracted span pairs.

\begin{figure}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{figures/figure1.pdf}
    \caption{Overall architecture. The shared encoder feeds token representations into two branches: a matrix head for quad extraction and an Opinion-Guided VA head. The VA head decomposes prediction into an opinion prior $\hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}}$ (from the opinion span alone) and a gated context residual $\Delta\mathbf{y}$ (from aspect-opinion interaction), combined via a learnable gate $\sigma(\mathbf{g})$.}
    \label{fig:architecture}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Base Architecture: Token-Pair Matrix Extraction}
\label{sec:base}

Given input text $\mathbf{x}$, we obtain contextualized representations $\mathbf{H} = (\mathbf{h}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{h}_n) \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$ from a pre-trained encoder (DeBERTa-v3-base~\cite{he2021debertav3}). The matrix head uses EfficientGlobalPointer~\cite{su2022globalpointer} with Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE)~\cite{su2024rope} to produce a score tensor $\mathbf{M} \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times n \times n}$, where $L$ is the number of label types encoding span-pair relationships:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{BA-BO}: aspect span boundaries $(i_a^{\text{start}}, i_a^{\text{end}})$,
    \item \texttt{EA-EO}: opinion span boundaries $(i_o^{\text{start}}, i_o^{\text{end}})$,
    \item \texttt{BA-EO-}$c$: aspect-opinion linking with category $c \in \mathcal{C}$.
\end{itemize}

Auxiliary sentence-level and token-level category classifiers provide multi-granularity supervision following~\cite{gao2023oneasqp}. FGM adversarial training~\cite{miyato2017fgm} is applied to the encoder for robustness.

\subsection{VA Prediction Architectures}
\label{sec:va}

We study three VA prediction architectures of increasing sophistication. For a decoded quadruplet with aspect span $(i_a^s, i_a^e)$ and opinion span $(i_o^s, i_o^e)$, we first compute span representations via mean pooling:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{h}_a = \frac{1}{i_a^e - i_a^s + 1}\sum_{j=i_a^s}^{i_a^e} \mathbf{h}_j, \quad
    \mathbf{h}_o = \frac{1}{i_o^e - i_o^s + 1}\sum_{j=i_o^s}^{i_o^e} \mathbf{h}_j
    \label{eq:pool}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection{Position VA (Baseline).}
The simplest approach predicts VA independently at each token position:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{pos}} = \sigma(\text{MLP}_{\text{pos}}(\mathbf{h}_t)) \cdot 8 + 1, \quad t \in \{i_a^s, i_o^s\}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma$ is the sigmoid function ensuring output in $[1, 9]$. At inference time, the VA value is read from the aspect start token $i_a^s$ of the matched quadruplet (or the opinion start token $i_o^s$ if the aspect is implicit). This approach cannot distinguish different VA values when one token participates in multiple quadruplets.

\subsubsection{Span-Pair VA (Ablation).}
To condition VA on the specific aspect-opinion pair, we concatenate span representations with their element-wise product:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{sp}} = \sigma\big(\text{MLP}_{\text{sp}}([\mathbf{h}_a;\; \mathbf{h}_o;\; \mathbf{h}_a \odot \mathbf{h}_o])\big) \cdot 8 + 1
    \label{eq:spanpair}
\end{equation}
This addresses quad ambiguity but treats both spans symmetrically, ignoring the asymmetric role of opinion expressions as primary sentiment carriers.

\subsubsection{Opinion-Guided VA (Proposed).}
Our key observation is that opinion expressions carry strong VA priors: ``\emph{delicious}'' implies high valence regardless of whether it modifies ``\emph{pizza}'' or ``\emph{sushi}'', while the aspect primarily modulates the intensity. We decompose VA prediction into:

\paragraph{Opinion Prior.} A dedicated head estimates base VA from the opinion span alone:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}} = \sigma\big(\text{MLP}_{\text{prior}}(\mathbf{h}_o)\big) \cdot 8 + 1
    \label{eq:prior}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Context Residual.} The aspect-opinion interaction produces a residual adjustment:
\begin{equation}
    \Delta\mathbf{y} = \text{MLP}_{\text{res}}([\mathbf{h}_a;\; \mathbf{h}_o;\; \mathbf{h}_a \odot \mathbf{h}_o])
    \label{eq:residual}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Gated Combination.} A learnable gate $\mathbf{g} \in \mathbb{R}^2$ (one per VA dimension) balances the two components:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{OG}} = \hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}} + \sigma(\mathbf{g}) \odot \Delta\mathbf{y}
    \label{eq:og}
\end{equation}

The final prediction is clamped to $[1, 9]$.

\paragraph{Handling Implicit Spans.}
DimASQP allows both aspect and opinion to be \texttt{NULL} (implicit). We follow One-ASQP~\cite{gao2023oneasqp} in using a designated \texttt{[NULL]} token (mapped to the \texttt{[SEP]} position) as a proxy representation for implicit spans:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{h}_a = \mathbf{h}_{\texttt{[SEP]}} \;\text{if aspect is implicit}, \quad
    \mathbf{h}_o = \mathbf{h}_{\texttt{[SEP]}} \;\text{if opinion is implicit}
    \label{eq:null}
\end{equation}
When opinion is implicit, the prior $\hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}}$ is derived from the sentence-level \texttt{[SEP]} representation rather than a specific opinion span. This is a principled fallback: the \texttt{[SEP]} token in DeBERTa aggregates sentence-level context, serving as a global sentiment proxy. In the \restaurant{} training set, 310 out of 2,036 quadruplets (15.2\%) have both aspect and opinion implicit; these are trained with the same loss formulation as explicit quads (no special weighting).

\paragraph{Optimization Intuition.}
We offer an informal motivation (not a formal guarantee) for why the decomposition may ease learning. In Span-Pair VA, the MLP must learn the full mapping from span representations to VA values. In Opinion-Guided VA, if the prior $\hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}}$ provides a reasonable initial estimate, the residual MLP only needs to learn a smaller correction $\Delta\mathbf{y}$. Empirically, the mean magnitude of learned residuals $|\Delta\mathbf{y}|$ is 1.23 (averaged across matched quads on the restaurant dev set), substantially smaller than the target range of $[1, 9]$, supporting that the prior absorbs a significant portion of the signal.

\subsection{Training Objective}
\label{sec:training}

The total loss combines four components:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L} = \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{\text{matrix}} + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{\text{cls}} + \lambda_3 \mathcal{L}_{\text{seq}} + \lambda_4 \mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}}
    \label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}

where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{matrix}}$ is the multi-label categorical cross-entropy for the matrix head~\cite{su2022globalpointer}, $\mathcal{L}_{\text{cls}}$ and $\mathcal{L}_{\text{seq}}$ are BCE losses for sentence-level and token-level category classifiers, and $\mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}}$ is the Smooth L1 loss for VA regression.

For the Opinion-Guided architecture, an auxiliary prior loss encourages the opinion prior to independently approximate VA values:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}}^{\text{OG}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}}(\hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{OG}}, \mathbf{y}) + \lambda_p \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{VA}}(\hat{\mathbf{y}}_{\text{prior}}, \mathbf{y})
    \label{eq:og_loss}
\end{equation}

where $\lambda_p$ weights the prior supervision. During training, gold span positions are used (teacher forcing); at inference, predicted spans from the matrix head are used.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:exp}

\subsection{Datasets and Setup}

We evaluate on the DimABSA 2026~\cite{dimabsa2026} English benchmark across two domains: \textbf{Restaurant} (1,530 train / 754 dev sentences, 1,177 dev quadruplets) and \textbf{Laptop} (2,934 train / 1,142 dev sentences, 1,601 dev quadruplets). Each sentence is annotated with quadruplets containing aspect terms, categories, opinion expressions, and continuous VA values.

We use DeBERTa-v3-base~\cite{he2021debertav3} as the encoder. Training uses AdamW~\cite{loshchilov2019adamw} with encoder learning rate $10^{-5}$, task head learning rate $3 \times 10^{-5}$, batch size 32 (4 $\times$ 8 gradient accumulation), mixed precision (FP16), and FGM adversarial training. Loss weights are $\lambda_1\!=\!1.0$, $\lambda_{2,3,4}\!=\!0.5$, with prior weight $\lambda_p\!=\!0.3$. Early stopping patience is 20 epochs. Each configuration is run with 3 seeds (42, 66, 123).

\paragraph{Evaluation Protocol.}
As the DimABSA 2026 shared task does not release test set gold labels at the time of writing, all reported results use the official development set. To avoid overfitting to the dev set, we adopt the following protocol: (1)~model selection uses only the best validation cF1 during training (early stopping); (2)~the matrix decoding threshold is selected via a single sweep on the dev set \emph{after} training completes, without any retraining; (3)~all hyperparameters ($\lambda_p$, learning rates, etc.) are fixed across all methods and seeds. The VA prediction head architecture is the \emph{only} variable across configurations, ensuring a controlled comparison.

\paragraph{Dedicated VA training is essential.}
The Centroid VA baseline (cF1\,$=$\,0.4103, VA\%\,$=$\,72.9\%) shows that per-category average VA values are grossly insufficient. Adding Position VA training raises cF1 by +0.105, establishing that VA prediction quality is the primary bottleneck for cF1.

\paragraph{Restaurant: VA quality drives cF1 improvement.}
On \restaurant{}, Opinion-Guided achieves the highest cF1 (0.5245), outperforming Position VA by +0.0089 and Span-Pair VA by +0.0065. The extraction-only F1\textsubscript{ext} is near-identical across all three (${\sim}$0.55), confirming that cF1 differences are driven entirely by VA prediction quality. The progression Position (93.8\%) $<$ Span-Pair (94.2\%) $<$ Opinion-Guided (94.4\%) on VA\% further supports this.

\paragraph{Laptop: VA quality improves but cF1 diverges.}
On \laptop{}, Opinion-Guided again achieves the highest VA\% (94.4 vs.\ 94.2 for Span-Pair and 93.7 for Position), confirming that the VA quality improvement is domain-independent. However, the end-to-end cF1 tells a different story: Span-Pair leads with 0.4043, while Opinion-Guided obtains 0.3979. The cause is not VA regression but extraction: F1\textsubscript{ext} drops from 0.4290 (Span-Pair) to 0.4214 (Opinion-Guided), indicating that the opinion prior head introduces a mild extraction interaction on this domain. Importantly, this interaction does not affect VA prediction quality---it affects the extraction pipeline that feeds into VA prediction. We discuss this domain sensitivity further in Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}.

\paragraph{Span context alone is insufficient.}
In both domains, Span-Pair VA---which conditions on the same span representations as Opinion-Guided---provides only marginal VA\% improvement over Position VA (+0.4 \restaurant{}, +0.5 \laptop{}). This confirms that span-level conditioning is necessary but not sufficient: the opinion prior decomposition is the key factor.

\paragraph{Scope of comparison.}
Our main table compares three internal VA head variants under strictly controlled conditions (same encoder, extraction head, and hyperparameters). The contribution is a \emph{VA prediction module}, not a complete system. Direct comparison with shared task systems (e.g., SCSG~\cite{scsg2026}, HITSZ-HLT~\cite{hitszSighan2024}) would conflate extraction architecture, pre-training, and VA prediction differences.

\subsection{Discussion: Why VA Gains Do Not Always Transfer to cF1}
\label{sec:discussion}

The laptop results reveal an instructive pattern: Opinion-Guided achieves the best VA prediction quality (VA\%\,$=$\,94.4, Euclid\,$=$\,0.627) but the lowest end-to-end cF1 (0.3979). This apparent contradiction arises because cF1 conflates two independent factors: extraction quality (whether the correct quads are found) and VA quality (how accurately VA is predicted for found quads).

On \restaurant{}, extraction-only F1\textsubscript{ext} is nearly identical across all three VA heads (${\sim}$0.55), so VA improvements translate directly to cF1 gains. On \laptop{}, F1\textsubscript{ext} varies: 0.4290 (Span-Pair) vs.\ 0.4214 (Opinion-Guided), a 0.8\% absolute drop. Since the VA head shares encoder gradients with the matrix head during joint training, the opinion prior's auxiliary loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{prior}}$) may introduce gradient interactions that slightly affect extraction on domains with larger and more diverse category taxonomies.

This finding has two practical implications. First, in deployment, extraction and VA prediction could be decoupled (e.g., freezing the extraction head after pre-training) to prevent such interactions. Second, it underscores the importance of reporting VA-specific metrics alongside cF1: the aggregate metric alone cannot distinguish extraction regressions from VA regressions, potentially masking genuine VA improvements.

\subsection{Parameter Efficiency and Statistical Reliability}
\label{sec:efficiency}

\paragraph{Parameter Overhead.}
The Opinion-Guided VA head adds only 197K parameters over Span-Pair VA (+0.11\% of the 184M encoder): a lightweight prior MLP (197K) and a 2-dimensional gate. The residual MLP (623K) is architecturally identical to the Span-Pair head. The total OG head is 821K parameters (0.45\% of encoder), confirming that the gain stems from the inductive bias, not increased capacity.

\paragraph{Statistical Reliability.}
We acknowledge that overall cF1 differences do not reach conventional statistical significance ($p < 0.05$) with only 3 seeds, and we do not claim otherwise. The cF1 metric compounds extraction variability with VA quality; with std $\approx 0.005\text{--}0.009$, larger sample sizes are needed to establish cF1 significance. We instead base our claims on three complementary lines of evidence:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{VA-specific significance}: on \restaurant{}, all pairwise VA comparisons reach $p < 0.05$; Span-Pair vs.\ OG reaches $p < 0.001$. On \laptop{}, Position vs.\ OG reaches $p < 0.05$; SP vs.\ OG is directionally consistent but $p > 0.05$ with $n\!=\!3$.
    \item \emph{Cross-domain consistency}: OG produces the lowest VA error in all 6 seed-domain combinations (3 seeds $\times$ 2 domains) without exception. The ordering Position $>$ SP $>$ OG is monotonic across the entire ablation chain.
    \item \emph{Extraction near-invariance}: on \restaurant{}, extraction F1 is near-identical across methods (${\sim}$0.55); on \laptop{}, a small extraction interaction exists (Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}), but VA metrics still isolate the targeted component.
\end{enumerate}

%==============================================================================
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We proposed Opinion-Guided VA prediction for the DimASQP task, decomposing sentiment estimation into an opinion-derived prior and a gated context residual. Systematic ablation across two domains showed that span-pair conditioning alone provides limited benefit, while the opinion prior decomposition yields consistent VA quality improvements on both restaurant ($-$9.6\% Euclidean distance, $p < 0.001$) and laptop ($-$11.8\%, $p < 0.05$). Learned gate analysis revealed a stable ${\sim}$60:40 residual-to-prior ratio, and per-category breakdowns showed that high-variability categories benefit most.

Cross-domain analysis revealed that VA quality improvements do not always translate to end-to-end cF1 gains: on restaurant, they do; on laptop, a mild extraction interaction offsets the VA improvement. This finding highlights the importance of decomposing cF1 into extraction and VA components for fair evaluation of VA prediction methods.

Limitations include the small number of evaluation seeds ($n\!=\!3$) and evaluation on development data only (test labels are not yet released). Future work includes disentangling architectural and supervision contributions via additional ablation, extending to multilingual settings, and exploring extraction-VA decoupling to prevent the gradient interactions observed on laptop.
